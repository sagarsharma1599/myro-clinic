What is Endoscopic Surgery?


Endoscopic surgery is a procedure that is used to remove tumors and other conditions that are too hard or impossible to access by traditional surgical methods. The surgeon inserts a fiber-optic telescope (goggles) through the mouth, cervix, or vagina and into the body. The scope sends an image to a TV monitor outside of the patient's body, allowing surgeons to perform their operations with greater precision and precision than ever before.

What is the purpose of endoscopy?

An endoscopy procedure is used to examine the interior of the body without having to make an incision. A tube called an endoscope is inserted through a small incision or body natural cavity such as the nose, ear, mouth, or rectum and allows for a direct look into specific areas of the body.
This type of examination is often used for patients who are experiencing difficulty swallowing or digesting foods.
Catheters with sterile instruments are also inserted into hollow organs to access them directly. The procedure is useful in many conditions, such as:
• Diagnose disorders
• Assist in surgery
• Clarifying on X-ray or CT scan
• Helicobacter pylori infection
• Zollinger-Ellison syndrome

The technique is usually done as an outpatient procedure and only causes a little pain. More detailed imaging is now possible all thanks to the advances in medicine and technology.

Difference Between Laparoscopy and Endoscopy

Both laparoscopy and endoscopy are minimally invasive surgical procedures. The main difference between the two is that laparoscopy is performed outside of the digestive tract on the abdominal cavity, while endoscopy is performed inside the digestive tract.
Laparoscopy and endoscopy are both procedures used to look inside the body. They are similar in that they both use a camera and light to see inside the body. The difference is that laparoscopy uses a small incision in the abdomen to insert the camera, while endoscopy uses a natural orifice, such as the mouth, to enter the body. Laparoscopy is used to look at organs in the abdomen, while endoscopy can be used to examine any part of the body.
Laparoscopy is a surgical intervention that allows the visualization of anatomy and organs during surgery. It is done through tiny incisions and long, narrow instruments (laparoscopic instruments).
Endoscopy is done through an endoscope, which has a light bulb fitted at the end. The endoscope can see in all directions inside the body or tube. The key difference between laparoscopy and endoscopy is that they are both related to their working mechanism and functioning mode.

When you might need one or both procedures?

If your doctor suspects you have a digestive problem, they may recommend a procedure called an endoscopy. Both procedures are usually performed as outpatient procedures, which means you can go home the same day. Laparoscopy is used to diagnose and treat conditions of the abdomen, such as Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, and diverticulitis. Endoscopy is used to diagnose and treat conditions of the gastrointestinal tract, such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and Barrett's esophagus.

How the procedures are performed

In Endoscopy surgery, a long thin tube with a camera known as endoscope is used. The endoscope is inserted in your body to look into the digestive tract. With the help of endoscope, your doctor may get a closer look of your abdomen.
After this, the surgeon will remove or treat the part of the organ which is troubling the patient. Once this is done the surgeon will close the incision. The patient after the surgery will be shifted to an observation room for 1 to 2 hour.
