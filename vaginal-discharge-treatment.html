What is Vaginal Discharge?

Vaginal discharge refers to the fluid that is produced by the glands in the vagina and cervix. It is a normal and essential part of the female reproductive system. The consistency, colour, and amount of vaginal discharge can vary depending on factors such as the menstrual cycle, hormonal changes, sexual arousal, pregnancy, and overall health.
Healthy vaginal discharge is usually clear or white and may be sticky or slippery in texture. It helps to keep the vagina clean and moist, as well as to prevent infections by flushing out bacteria and dead cells. The amount of discharge can vary throughout the menstrual cycle, being relatively low immediately after the period and increasing as ovulation approaches.
However, there are certain instances when vaginal discharge may indicate an underlying issue. If the discharge changes in colour, consistency, or smell, or is accompanied by other symptoms such as itching, burning, pain, or irritation, it may be a sign of infection or other health concerns. Some common causes of abnormal vaginal discharge include yeast infections, bacterial vaginosis, sexually transmitted infections (such as chlamydia, gonorrhoea, or trichomoniasis), hormonal imbalances, and certain medical conditions.
If you are experiencing abnormal vaginal discharge or are concerned about your vaginal health, it is recommended to consult a healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment, if necessary. They can perform a physical examination, ask about your symptoms and medical history, and may conduct further tests or collect samples for laboratory analysis if needed.

Types of Vaginal Discharge?


Vaginal discharge can vary in colour, consistency, and smell, which can provide clues about the underlying cause. Here are some common types of vaginal discharge and their potential meanings:

1. Clear and Stretchy: This type of discharge is often described as "egg white" in consistency. It is typically seen around the time of ovulation and is considered normal. It indicates increased fertility and is associated with the release of an egg from the ovary.
2. White and Thick: A white and thick discharge with a consistency similar to cottage cheese may indicate a yeast infection, also known as candidiasis. Other symptoms include itching, redness, and irritation in the vaginal area.
3. Grayish or Fishy Odor: A greyish discharge with a strong fishy odour may be a sign of bacterial vaginosis (BV). BV occurs due to an imbalance of the vaginal bacteria. It is important to note that not all cases of BV have noticeable symptoms.
4. Yellow or Green: Yellow or green discharge can indicate an infection, such as trichomoniasis or a sexually transmitted infection (STI) like gonorrhoea. These infections often cause other symptoms like itching, pain during urination or sex, and inflammation.
5. Brown or Bloody: Brown or bloody discharge can occur during various times in a woman's menstrual cycle. It can be normal and related to the shedding of the uterine lining during menstruation or spotting between periods. However, if the discharge is accompanied by severe pain or lasts for an extended period, it is advisable to seek medical attention.

It's important to remember that everyone's body is unique, and the appearance of vaginal discharge can vary. If you notice any significant changes in your vaginal discharge, such as a sudden increase in amount, a foul odour, or accompanying symptoms, it's recommended to consult a healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment.

When to Visit a doctor:

It is advisable to visit a doctor if you experience any of the following concerning symptoms or changes in vaginal discharge:

1. Abnormal discharge: If you notice a sudden change in colour, consistency, or smell of your vaginal discharge, such as a strong fishy odour, green or yellow discharge, or discharge that appears frothy or cottage cheese-like.
2. Itching, burning, or irritation:If you experience persistent itching, burning, redness, or irritation in the vaginal area, it could be a sign of an infection or other underlying issue.
3. Pain or discomfort: If you have pain or discomfort in the vaginal area, especially during urination, sexual intercourse, or during menstruation, it is important to seek medical attention.
4. Abnormal bleeding: If you experience heavy or prolonged vaginal bleeding, bleeding between periods, or bleeding after menopause, it is essential to consult a doctor.
5. Sexually transmitted infection (STI): If you suspect you may have been exposed to an STI or if you experience symptoms such as unusual discharge, sores, or genital warts, it is crucial to get tested and seek appropriate treatment.
6. Concerns during pregnancy: If you are pregnant and experience any changes in vaginal discharge, pain, bleeding, or other symptoms, it is important to consult your healthcare provider to ensure the well-being of both you and your baby.
